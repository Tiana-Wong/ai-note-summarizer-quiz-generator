### **Question 1:** 
According to the lecture, what is the fundamental difference between ethics and morals?
- **A)** Ethics is the same as morals
- **B)** Ethics = Morals + Reasoning
- **C)** Morals are religious while ethics are philosophical
- **D)** Ethics only apply in professional settings

**Correct Answer: B** - Ethics = Morals + Reasoning. Ethics requires reasoning and justification, while morality is simply adhering to what society or religion describes as right.

---

### **Question 2:**
What are the three main branches of ethics discussed in the lecture?
- **A)** Personal, Professional, and Applied
- **B)** Meta-Ethics, Normative Ethics, and Applied Ethics
- **C)** Deontological, Teleological, and Virtue
- **D)** Descriptive, Prescriptive, and Analytical

**Correct Answer: B** - Meta-Ethics (ethics about ethics), Normative Ethics (determining if actions are right/wrong), and Applied Ethics (practical branch for specific fields).

### **Question 3:**

- **A)** Ethical Egoism
- **B)** Deontological Ethics
- **C)** Utilitarianism

**Correct Answer: C** - Utilitarianism holds that morality is about producing the greatest balance of benefits over harms for everyone affected.

## ðŸ“š Key Concepts to Remember:

1. **Ethics vs. Morals**: Ethics requires reasoning; morals are just following norms
2. **Three Main Ethical Branches**:
   - Meta-Ethics: About the definition of right/wrong
   - Normative Ethics: Determining if actions are right/wrong
   - Applied Ethics: Practical ethics in specific fields

3. **Major Ethical Theories**:
   - **Teleological/Consequentialist**: Focus on outcomes (Utilitarianism, Egoism)
   - **Deontological/Nonconsequentialist**: Focus on duty/rules (Kant)
   - **Virtue Ethics**: Focus on character (Aristotle, Confucius)
   - **Rights-based**: Focus on justice (Rawls)
   - **Love-based**: Agape principle

4. **Decision-Making Tools**: The Potter Box helps with ethical decision-making through facts, values, principles, and loyalties.
