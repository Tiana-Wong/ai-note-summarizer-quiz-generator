## Quiz: 30 Comprehensive Multiple-Choice Questions

### **Question 1:**
What are the two fundamental facets of the Rule of Law?
- **A)** Justice and Fairness
- **B)** Principle of Legality and Principle of Equality
- **C)** Power and Authority
- **D)** Democracy and Rights

**Correct Answer: B** - The Rule of Law consists of: (1) Principle of Legality - power derived from law, and (2) Principle of Equality - all persons subject to law regardless of status.

---

### **Question 2:**
What is the Principle of Legality?
- **A)** Laws must be written in legal language
- **B)** Government power must be derived from law; no one can act without legal justification
- **C)** All laws must apply equally
- **D)** The legal system must be perfect

**Correct Answer: B** - The Principle of Legality means government power is derived from law and legislation, and anyone affected can resort to court if there's no legal justification for an act.

---

### **Question 3:**
In the HKSAR system, who is bound by the principle that no legal action can be taken without legal justification?
- **A)** Only ordinary citizens
- **B)** Only government officials
- **C)** No one, the Chief Executive is exempt
- **D)** Everyone, including the Chief Executive

**Correct Answer: D** - At the heart of HKSAR's system is the principle that NO ONE, including the Chief Executive, can do an act without legal justification.

**Correct Answer: B** - If an act is found unlawful, a court may rule it invalid and of no legal effect, and compensation may be ordered in favor of the affected person.

## ðŸ“š Key Concepts to Remember:

1. **Rule of Law vs Rule by Law:**
   - Rule of Law: Government bound by law, accountable to courts
   - Rule by Law: Government uses law as tool for control

2. **Two Fundamental Facets:**
   - Principle of Legality: Power derived from law
   - Principle of Equality: All subject to law regardless of status

3. **Separation of Powers:**
   - Executive, Legislative, Judicial branches
   - Each checks and balances others
   - Prevents concentration of power

4. **Judicial Independence:**
   - Essential for impartial rulings
   - Protects from executive interference
   - Ensures Rule of Law

5. **Administrative Law:**
   - Guidelines for executive power
   - Ensures discretionary power is restricted
   - Protects against arbitrary government action
